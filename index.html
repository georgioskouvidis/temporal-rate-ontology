Overview

Interpretive framework Empirically conservative

TRO targets a recurrent inference in pedagogy and informal foundations discourse: the slide from geometry as an encoding to geometry as an ontological ground. The framework argues that geometry can be indispensable for representation while remaining ontologically non-fundamental.

The project develops: (i) a comparative taxonomy of non-geometric primitives (causal, process, informational, event-based), (ii) a programmatic account of representational pressure toward spatial encoding, and (iii) a focused application to the problem of time in canonical quantum gravity.

What TRO is

  • A constraint-based interpretation of physics formalisms
  • A diagnosis of representational/ontological conflation
  • A comparative proposal: temporal succession as minimally committal

What TRO is not

  • Not a new physical theory
  • Not a derivation of the Born rule or measurement resolution
  • Not a constructive quantum gravity program (explicitly)

At a glance

Plain-language

One-sentence pitch:
Temporal Rate Ontology treats ordered temporal succession as ontologically primitive and interprets spacetime geometry as a representational encoding of how multiple rate-differentiated processes remain coordinated.

How to read the papers:
Start with the comparative paper (taxonomy + constraints), then the spatial paper (programmatic representational pressure), then the problem-of-time paper (high-impact application to canonical quantum gravity).
